Motivation

I started my professional journey as a KYC analyst and a consultant for the youth court. It quickly became clear that I hadn’t yet found the right career path. Though my education is in a different field, I've always had a deep interest in Tech and IT. Opportunely, a friend invited me to interview for a test engineering role. At the time, I had no background in software testing, but I quickly immersed myself in studying the theory behind this subject and found it incredibly engaging.

Though I didn’t land the job due to my lack of experience, the process inspired me to earn an ISTQB certification and continue exploring software testing in-depth. I began networking with professionals in the field, and they encouraged me to look into Campus 19, a free coding bootcamp. Initially, the prospect of learning to code was intimidating-developers had always seemed like the magicians of our time, and I questioned if I could keep up. Still, I decided to take the leap, and in September, I dedicated myself fully to learning programming.

The intensive month-long “Piscine” was eye-opening. Coding was as challenging as I’d imagined, but also very rewarding. I studied alongside people from all backgrounds, tackling complex problems without any teachers to guide us. This experience showed me that with enough dedication and perseverance, I could build these new skills—and, for the first time, I felt certain about the direction I wanted my career to take.

Since October, I have been continuing my studies on my own through The Odin Project, though I miss the interaction with other students . This journey has been transformative, and I’m excited to continue developing my skills and connecting with others in the tech community.
